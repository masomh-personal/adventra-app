# Definition of Ready (DoR)

1. Title
    - The backlog item has a clear and concise title that describes the feature, bug, or task.
2. User story opening sentence
    - The item follows a structured format such as: “As a [user role], I want/need [feature or capability], so that [benefit].”
3. Additional details
    - The backlog item includes necessary context and technical information for developers and testers.
    - Links to relevant documentation (e.g., design mockups, requirements, or architectural decisions) are provided.
4. Estimated in story points
    - The team has assigned story points to the item based on its complexity and effort.
5. Design/Mockups (If Applicable)
    - Design specifications, wireframes, or mockups are provided if applicable.
    - The design addresses specific UI/UX requirements where needed.
6. Product Owner Approval
    - There is a shared understanding of the expected outcome between the Product Owner, development team, and stakeholders.
    - The Product Owner has reviewed and confirmed that the item is ready to be worked on.
7. Identified Dependencies
    - All necessary dependencies (e.g., third-party integrations, API requirements) are identified and addressed.
8. Dependicies & Prerequsites 
    - Any prerequisite work (e.g., database setup, infrastructure readiness) is completed before development begins.
    - Any required external services, APIs, or components are accessible and available for development.
